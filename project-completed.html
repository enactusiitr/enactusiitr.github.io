Projects Completed

Project ReStove

Project Restove is an innovative portable biogas solution designed to convert organic waste into clean cooking fuel, offering a sustainable and eco-friendly alternative to traditional cooking methods. This initiative plays a crucial role in reducing indoor air pollution, minimizing waste, and promoting energy self-sufficiency in rural communities.

Project Khoj

With overcrowding in mainstream tourist destinations and a lack of exploration of lesser-known cultural sites, Khoj focuses on identifying and promoting hidden tourism gems. By researching villages and unexplored locations with tourism potential, the project aims to attract visitors to new destinations while also generating economic opportunities for local communities.

Project Anushruti

India has a significant population of approximately 63 million individuals who are deaf or hard of hearing (DHH), yet there are only 304 certified Indian Sign Language (ISL) interpreters in the country. This creates a major communication barrier in education, employment, and essential services. Anushruti aims to bridge this gap by developing an AI-based real-time ISL-to-text converter that can interpret sign language through video input and convert it into text.

Project Mg Lamps

Many tribal and roadside communities rely on kerosene lamps for lighting, exposing themselves to hazardous fumes and fire risks. Mg Lamps provides a safer and more sustainable alternative by developing an aluminium-air cell-based lamp that can generate up to 30 hours of energy using just salt and water. This eliminates the need for combustible fuels while providing consistent lighting.
